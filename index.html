    Level Up

    Over the last couple of years, games have become more and more common and widespread, especially throughout the younger generation. Today it’s used in everything from entertainment to learning experiences. Therefore, we need to look at what is often called “Serious Gaming ”.

    We will create a product that focuses on learning something, but at the same time focuses on the playful aspect out of it. In recent years, the IXD industry has begun to use elements known from the gaming industry in its products (Gamification). Today, game design theory is used on the web, in e-learning and app development.

    We will focus on what motivates a player in a ”Serious Game” to learn about a topic. In this project we were tasked with developing a physical game that must be able to be played in groups in high schools. We worked iteratively on a game concept. We had to teach ourselves to define problems and use game theory together with interaction design and other subject areas to make this game. By using iterative processes we are supposed to should solve one and one problem at a time and always make sure that we have progression in on our game.

    AFTNPSTN

    Aftenposten is Norway's largest subscription newspaper with just over 250,000 subscriptions, of which more than 115,000 are purely digital subscriptions. But established media have an age problem.

    They struggle to remain relevant to a generation that has grown up with distractions and wires from digital media. But young people are still interested in knowing - reading, hearing and seeing - what is happening around them In this project, we were to prototype and present an interactive solution for Aftenposten that would ensure that young people between 18-25 years visit aftenposten.no regularly to get news and information about what is happening around them.

    Good interaction design is the result of a thorough process and requires that one designs and tests a variety of different solution proposals. The process must be documented in an appropriate way, with clear arguments for the choice of methods, as well as design choices made along the way.
